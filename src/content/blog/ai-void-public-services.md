We often hear praises about digital transformation in our public services, with platforms like e-Devlet and e-Nabız held up as prime examples. But let's face the reality: these platforms are little more than digital filing cabinets. They lack the intelligence required to actually solve problems.

Take the healthcare system as an example. When you log into e-Nabız, you are greeted with raw data, lab results, and past prescriptions that you likely cannot interpret. You search for answers but find none. Then, you go to a hospital only to face the infamous "five-minute examination." A doctor has barely enough time to look at you, let alone review your extensive medical history. It is a deeply flawed system that exhausts the physicians and leaves the patients feeling neglected.

This is where the absence of AI becomes painfully obvious. We have the technology right now to turn e-Nabız into a proactive assistant. AI could seamlessly analyze a patient's history, flag potential risks, cross-reference past medications, and present a clear, concise summary to the doctor the moment the patient enters the room. It could give doctors their time back — time to actually care for the human being sitting in front of them.

Instead, we force brilliant medical professionals to act as data entry clerks. The technology to revolutionize this efficiency exists today, but the people who know how to build it are sitting on the sidelines, watching a system collapse under its own weight because those in charge lack the vision to implement real change.
